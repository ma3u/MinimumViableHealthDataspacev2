The regulation ships a gap

  • EHDS (Reg. 2025/327, Ch. IV): secondary use runs on an HDAB data permit + a citizen right to opt out — no reason needed
  • eIDAS 2.0 (Reg. 2024/1183) ships the wallet, PID and EAA — the missing citizen-facing channel
  • The gap: no standardised, citizen-held, machine-verifiable opt-out + consent channel
  • Default fallback = a central opt-out registry: a honeypot the citizen never really controls

Regulation & privacy-by-design

  • eIDAS 2.0 (2024/1183) → the attestation: PID + EAA, selective disclosure, data minimisation
  • EHDS (2025/327, Ch. IV) → HDAB permit + opt-out: MedReg DE issues the permit; the citizen's opt-out is honoured at the gate
  • GDPR → HDAB assesses the Art. 6 basis; Art. 7(3) withdrawal where consent applies; the EHDS opt-out needs no reason (beyond Art. 21)
  • Honest scope: enforced at the access boundary today; cryptographic status-list (IETF Token Status List, draft) is roadmap
